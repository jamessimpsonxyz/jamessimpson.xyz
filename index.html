James Simpson

Builder & investor in crypto. Writing on technology, geopolitics, and power.

I build and invest in technologies that expand freedom and trust.

I’m fascinated by how systems of value and ownership evolve. I believe crypto can make the internet and finance more private, sovereign, transparent, and inclusive.

I’m based in Paris, where I completed a master’s in International Security at Sciences Po and worked with UNESCO on AI bias. I also write with the Spykman Centre on geopolitics, technology, and the shifting architecture of global power.

Previously I founded and sold mStable, an early DeFi protocol built in Berlin, and have since invested in 20+ early-stage ventures across crypto and frontier tech.

Outside of work, I love acting and the mountains.

Projects

Split — A protocol for splitting tokens into constituent components. Born from conversations with Francesco Agosti, Richard Galvin, and Alex Erikstrup that became a whitepaper, a prototype, and a usable product. The MVP was built during the three-week ETHGlobal hackathon and won the Compound sponsor prize.

mStable — Conceived at Apollo Capital with Henrik Andersson to address fragmentation and risk in stablecoins. Built products to unify stable assets, generate yield, and enhance systemic resilience. Acquired via a community-led decentralised auction; now continues under dHEDGE, building on Pendle.
